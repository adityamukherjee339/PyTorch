Classification is about predicting discrete categories. PyTorch provides specialized loss functions and layers for these tasks.

## Binary vs Multi-class

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Task</th>
      <th>Output Layer Activation</th>
      <th>Loss Function</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>Binary</td>
      <td>Sigmoid</td>
      <td><code>nn.BCEWithLogitsLoss()</code></td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Multi-class</td>
      <td>Softmax (implicit)</td>
      <td><code>nn.CrossEntropyLoss()</code></td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

<Callout type="warn">
PyTorch's `nn.CrossEntropyLoss` and `nn.BCEWithLogitsLoss` expect raw logits as inputs, NOT probabilities. Do not apply a softmax or sigmoid layer before these loss functions!
</Callout>
